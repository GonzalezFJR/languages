VERBOS IRREGULARES COMUNES EN INGLÉS
=====================================

Los verbos irregulares en inglés no siguen el patrón estándar de añadir
"-ed" para formar el pasado simple y el participio pasado. Aquí presentamos
los más frecuentes agrupados por similitud fonética.

──────────────────────────────────────────
GRUPO 1: Infinitivo = Pasado = Participio
──────────────────────────────────────────
cut     – cut     – cut      (cortar)
put     – put     – put      (poner)
hit     – hit     – hit      (golpear)
let     – let     – let      (dejar)
set     – set     – set      (establecer)
shut    – shut    – shut     (cerrar)
read    – read    – read     (leer) *pronunciación cambia: /riːd/ → /rɛd/

──────────────────────────────────────────
GRUPO 2: Pasado = Participio (≠ Infinitivo)
──────────────────────────────────────────
bring   – brought – brought  (traer)
buy     – bought  – bought   (comprar)
catch   – caught  – caught   (atrapar)
teach   – taught  – taught   (enseñar)
think   – thought – thought  (pensar)
say     – said    – said     (decir)
sell    – sold    – sold     (vender)
tell    – told    – told     (contar/decir)
have    – had     – had      (tener)
make    – made    – made     (hacer/fabricar)
meet    – met     – met      (conocer/reunirse)
find    – found   – found    (encontrar)
hold    – held    – held     (sostener)
hear    – heard   – heard    (oír)
keep    – kept    – kept     (mantener/guardar)
leave   – left    – left     (salir/dejar)
lose    – lost    – lost     (perder)
sit     – sat     – sat      (sentarse)
stand   – stood   – stood    (estar de pie)
understand – understood – understood (entender)
win     – won     – won      (ganar)

──────────────────────────────────────────
GRUPO 3: Los tres formas son distintas
──────────────────────────────────────────
be      – was/were – been    (ser/estar)
begin   – began   – begun   (comenzar)
break   – broke   – broken  (romper)
choose  – chose   – chosen  (elegir)
come    – came    – come    (venir)
do      – did     – done    (hacer)
drink   – drank   – drunk   (beber)
drive   – drove   – driven  (conducir)
eat     – ate     – eaten   (comer)
fall    – fell    – fallen  (caer)
fly     – flew    – flown   (volar)
forget  – forgot  – forgotten (olvidar)
get     – got     – gotten  (obtener/llegar a ser)
give    – gave    – given   (dar)
go      – went    – gone    (ir)
grow    – grew    – grown   (crecer)
know    – knew    – known   (saber/conocer)
ride    – rode    – ridden  (montar)
ring    – rang    – rung    (sonar/llamar)
rise    – rose    – risen   (levantarse/subir)
run     – ran     – run     (correr)
see     – saw     – seen    (ver)
show    – showed  – shown   (mostrar)
sing    – sang    – sung    (cantar)
speak   – spoke   – spoken  (hablar)
steal   – stole   – stolen  (robar)
swim    – swam    – swum    (nadar)
take    – took    – taken   (tomar/coger)
throw   – threw   – thrown  (lanzar)
wake    – woke    – woken   (despertar)
wear    – wore    – worn    (llevar/vestir)
write   – wrote   – written (escribir)

──────────────────────────────────────────
NOTA SOBRE EL USO
──────────────────────────────────────────
En inglés americano, "gotten" es el participio estándar de "get".
En inglés británico se usa "got" para ambas formas (pasado y participio).

Ejemplo de uso:
  I have gotten better at English.  (Am.)
  I have got better at English.     (Br.)
