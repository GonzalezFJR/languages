COMMON CHINESE RADICALS & CHARACTERS
=======================================

Chinese characters are built from components called radicals
(部首, bùshǒu). Learning the most common radicals helps you
guess the meaning of unfamiliar characters.


1. BASIC PICTOGRAPHIC CHARACTERS

   人 (rén)  — person (looks like a person walking)
   大 (dà)   — big (person with arms stretched wide)
   小 (xiǎo) — small (three small strokes)
   山 (shān) — mountain (three peaks)
   水 (shuǐ) — water (flowing drops)
   火 (huǒ)  — fire (flames)
   日 (rì)   — sun / day (originally a circle with a dot)
   月 (yuè)  — moon / month (crescent shape)
   木 (mù)   — tree / wood (trunk with branches and roots)
   口 (kǒu)  — mouth (a square opening)


2. MOST USEFUL RADICALS

   亻 (person radical) — used in: 你 (nǐ, you), 他 (tā, he),
      们 (men, plural marker), 做 (zuò, to do)

   氵 (water radical) — used in: 河 (hé, river), 海 (hǎi, sea),
      洗 (xǐ, to wash), 汤 (tāng, soup)

   女 (woman radical) — used in: 妈 (mā, mother), 她 (tā, she),
      好 (hǎo, good — woman + child = good!)

   讠 (speech radical) — used in: 说 (shuō, to speak), 话 (huà, words),
      请 (qǐng, please), 谢 (xiè, to thank)

   食/饣 (food radical) — used in: 饭 (fàn, rice/meal),
      饿 (è, hungry), 饮 (yǐn, to drink)

   心/忄 (heart radical) — used in: 想 (xiǎng, to think/miss),
      快 (kuài, fast/happy), 忙 (máng, busy)


3. HOW CHARACTERS WORK

   Most characters combine a radical (meaning hint) with a
   phonetic component (sound hint):

   妈 mā (mother) = 女 (woman) + 马 mǎ (horse — gives sound "ma")
   请 qǐng (please) = 讠 (speech) + 青 qīng (blue — gives sound)
   洗 xǐ (wash) = 氵 (water) + 先 xiān (first — partial sound hint)

   ⚠️ Sound hints are approximate — tones often differ!


4. NUMBERS

   一 yī    (one)       六 liù   (six)
   二 èr    (two)       七 qī    (seven)
   三 sān   (three)     八 bā    (eight)
   四 sì    (four)      九 jiǔ   (nine)
   五 wǔ    (five)      十 shí   (ten)

   百 bǎi (hundred)     千 qiān (thousand)     万 wàn (ten thousand)

   ⚠️ Chinese counts in units of 10,000 (万), not 1,000.
   So 50,000 = 五万 (wǔ wàn), literally "five ten-thousands".


5. STROKE ORDER RULES

   Characters must be written following specific stroke orders:
   1. Top to bottom: 三 (sān) — three horizontal strokes, top first
   2. Left to right: 川 (chuān) — three vertical strokes, left first
   3. Horizontal before vertical: 十 (shí) — horizontal, then vertical
   4. Outside before inside: 月 (yuè) — outer frame, then inner strokes
   5. Close the frame last: 国 (guó) — bottom stroke of 口 goes last
