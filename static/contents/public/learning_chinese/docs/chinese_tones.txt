CHINESE TONES: THE ESSENTIAL GUIDE
=====================================

Mandarin Chinese is a tonal language — the same syllable pronounced
with different tones has completely different meanings. Mastering
tones is the single most important step for comprehension.


1. THE FOUR TONES (plus neutral)

   1st tone (ˉ) — HIGH AND FLAT
   Hold a high, steady pitch. Like singing a note.
   mā (妈) = mother

   2nd tone (ˊ) — RISING
   Start mid-low and rise up. Like asking "huh?" in English.
   má (麻) = hemp / numb

   3rd tone (ˇ) — DIP (falling-rising)
   Start mid, dip down, then rise. In connected speech,
   often just goes low without the rise.
   mǎ (马) = horse

   4th tone (ˋ) — FALLING
   Start high and drop sharply. Like saying "No!" firmly.
   mà (骂) = to scold

   5th tone (neutral) — LIGHT AND SHORT
   Unstressed, short. Pitch depends on previous tone.
   ma (吗) = question particle

   ⚠️ Same sound "ma" = mother, hemp, horse, scold, or question
   depending on tone. Tones are NOT optional!


2. TONE CHANGE RULES

   Third tone sandhi:
   When two 3rd tones appear in sequence, the first one
   changes to 2nd tone:
   你好 nǐ hǎo → actually pronounced "ní hǎo"

   一 (yī) tone changes:
   Before 4th tone: yí (changes to 2nd tone)
     一个 yí gè (one [item])
   Before 1st/2nd/3rd tone: yì (changes to 4th tone)
     一天 yì tiān (one day)

   不 (bù) tone change:
   Before 4th tone: bú
     不是 bú shì (is not)


3. INITIALS (CONSONANT SOUNDS)

   Most are similar to English, except:
   zh — like "j" in "juice" but with tongue curled back
   ch — like "ch" in "church" but tongue curled back
   sh — like "sh" in "she" but tongue curled back
   r  — between English "r" and French "j"
   z  — like "ds" in "beds"
   c  — like "ts" in "cats"
   q  — like "ch" in "cheese" (NOT like English "q")
   x  — like "sh" but with tongue flat (NOT like English "x")

   ⚠️ q, x, j only pair with ü (written as u after them).
   "qu" is pronounced "chü", not "kw"!


4. FINALS (VOWEL SOUNDS)

   a   — open "ah" as in "father"
   o   — "aw" as in British "saw"
   e   — "uh" as in "duh" (not like English "e"!)
   i   — "ee" as in "see"
   u   — "oo" as in "food"
   ü   — round lips for "oo", say "ee" (French "u")

   Common combinations:
   ai  — "eye"          ei  — "ay" as in "day"
   ao  — "ow" as in "cow"   ou  — "oh"
   an  — "ahn"          en  — "un" as in "fun"
   ang — "ahng"         eng — "ung"
   ian — "yen"          uan — "wahn"


5. PRACTICE MINIMAL PAIRS

   Try distinguishing these:
   māo (猫 cat) vs. máo (毛 fur) vs. mào (帽 hat)
   shī (诗 poetry) vs. shí (十 ten) vs. shǐ (史 history) vs. shì (是 is)
   bāo (包 bag) vs. bǎo (宝 treasure) vs. bào (报 newspaper)
