SPANISH VERB CONJUGATION: A QUICK GUIDE
=========================================

Spanish verbs are classified by their infinitive endings:
-AR (hablar), -ER (comer), -IR (vivir). Each follows a
predictable conjugation pattern in regular forms.


1. PRESENT TENSE (Presente de indicativo)

   -AR verbs: HABLAR (to speak)
   yo hablo          nosotros hablamos
   tú hablas         vosotros habláis
   él/ella habla     ellos/ellas hablan

   -ER verbs: COMER (to eat)
   yo como           nosotros comemos
   tú comes          vosotros coméis
   él/ella come      ellos/ellas comen

   -IR verbs: VIVIR (to live)
   yo vivo           nosotros vivimos
   tú vives          vosotros vivís
   él/ella vive      ellos/ellas viven

   ⚠️ Note: -ER and -IR verbs share most endings.


2. COMMON IRREGULAR VERBS (Present)

   SER (to be — identity/characteristics)
   yo soy, tú eres, él es, nosotros somos, ellos son

   ESTAR (to be — location/temporary states)
   yo estoy, tú estás, él está, nosotros estamos, ellos están

   TENER (to have)
   yo tengo, tú tienes, él tiene, nosotros tenemos, ellos tienen

   IR (to go)
   yo voy, tú vas, él va, nosotros vamos, ellos van

   ⚠️ SER vs ESTAR is one of the trickiest concepts for English
   speakers. SER = permanent. ESTAR = temporary/location.
   "Ella es bonita" (She is pretty — permanent trait)
   "Ella está cansada" (She is tired — temporary state)


3. PAST TENSES

   Pretérito (Simple Past) — completed actions:
   hablé, hablaste, habló, hablamos, hablaron

   Imperfecto (Imperfect) — ongoing/habitual past:
   hablaba, hablabas, hablaba, hablábamos, hablaban

   EN: "I spoke to her yesterday." → Hablé con ella ayer. (pretérito)
   EN: "I used to speak Spanish as a child." → Hablaba español de niño. (imperfecto)


4. PRONOUNS AND WORD ORDER

   Subject pronouns are OPTIONAL in Spanish (the verb ending
   tells you who):
   "Hablo español" = "I speak Spanish" (no need for "yo")

   Object pronouns go BEFORE the verb:
   "Lo veo" = "I see him/it"
   "Te quiero" = "I love you"


5. GENDER AND ARTICLES

   All nouns have grammatical gender (masculine or feminine):
   el libro (the book — masc.)
   la casa (the house — fem.)

   Generally: -o endings = masculine, -a endings = feminine
   ⚠️ Exceptions: el día (the day), la mano (the hand)
